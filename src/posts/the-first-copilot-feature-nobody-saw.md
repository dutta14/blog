---
title: The first Copilot feature nobody saw
date: January 11, 2024
excerpt: We shipped a voice assistant into Outlook in 2021 that almost no one used.
tags: ai-products
---

We shipped a voice assistant into Outlook in 2021 that almost no one used. The numbers were small enough that in another context they would have been a failure, and we discussed them in reviews with the careful neutrality of people who had decided not to be discouraged. The model was early. The product was early. We were early in the way that feels lonely until it doesn't.

What we were really doing, I think, was learning what an LLM product was. There was no playbook. The research was ahead of the engineering, the engineering was ahead of the product thinking, the product thinking was ahead of the users, and everybody was moving as fast as they could in a direction that felt right but wasn't yet proven. I made calls in those two years that I would make differently now. I also made some that I think were exactly right, and it took three more years to see which were which.

The feature is gone now, folded into something larger and better. Sometimes I open Copilot in Outlook and use it, and I think about the team we had in those early months, the arguments in design reviews, the specific people who wrote code that no longer exists. It is a strange thing to build a foundation for something you cannot see yet.
