ChatGPT launched on a Wednesday in November 2022, and by Friday my calendar had three new recurring meetings on it. That is not a complaint. It is just what the inside of a large company looks like when the external world catches up to something you have been building toward for two years.

My team had been working on LLM features in Outlook since 2021, when the models were expensive and slow and impressive in a way that was hard to explain to anyone who hadn't used one. We had our own internal names for things. We had a small group of users trying it, giving us feedback that was specific and useful and mostly unprintable in formal documents. Then a research lab released a chatbot to the public, and within a week everyone's uncle had an opinion about AI. The emails from leadership that December had a different quality to them. Faster. More urgent. One of them contained the phrase "this is our moment," which I had not received from a vice president before.

What I remember most clearly is not the excitement, which was real, but the strange doubling of it, the feeling of watching something you have been part of privately become a subject everyone was suddenly discussing in public. The product we had been quietly building was now the thing everyone wanted. That is a good problem to have. It is still a disorienting one.
