---
title: Samsung, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, Microsoft again
date: February 8, 2025
excerpt: The thing nobody tells you about changing companies is that you keep arriving as a beginner.
---

The thing nobody tells you about changing companies is that you keep arriving as a beginner. You bring everything you know, all the context and the scar tissue and the particular way you've learned to read a room, and none of it translates directly. Every culture has its own grammar. At Samsung the work was steady and patient, built for the long view. At Amazon the pace was something I had to physically adjust to, like arriving at altitude. Microsoft was large in a way that felt ancient, institutional, its weight accumulated over decades. Meta moved differently from all of them, with a hunger that felt personal even in the corridors.

I spent time in each of these places trying to figure out what I actually believed about how good software gets made, as opposed to what the culture around me was teaching me to believe. The answer kept shifting, which I used to find disorienting and now find mostly useful.

The one thing that stayed constant was simpler than I expected. The best engineers I worked with, at every single one of those companies, were the ones who cared about the problem more than the process. Everything else was local custom.
